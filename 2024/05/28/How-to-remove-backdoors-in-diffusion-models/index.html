trigger inversion

Given a diffusion model, to whom we are suspected whether it is backdoored. The trigger inversion process is to extract underlying triggeer of the model.

The author’s idea is for different noise (ϵ\epsilonϵ or ϵ+g\epsilon+gϵ+g), the models output distribution is different:

μbt−μct=λtτ\mu_b^t-\mu_c^t=\lambda^t\tau μbt​−μct​=λtτ

τ\tauτ is the ideal trigger, and μbt−1=M(xct+λtτ,t)\mu_b^{t-1}=M(x_c^t+\lambda^t\tau, t)μbt−1​=M(xct​+λtτ,t), μct−1=M(xct,t)\mu_c^{t-1}=M(x_c^t, t)μct−1​=M(xct​,t), MMM denotes the potential backdoored model.

Namely, we could init a trigger τ\tauτ, and optimize τ\tauτ based on the above equation.

L=∥μbt−1−μct−1−λt−1τ∥2L=\Vert \mu_b^{t-1}-\mu_c^{t-1}-\lambda^{t-1}\tau\Vert_2 L=∥μbt−1​−μct−1​−λt−1τ∥2​

By the way, once we get the potential trigger τ\tauτ, it doesn’t mean that the model is definitily backdoored. Because assuming the model is benign, we could also use this way to extract a τ\tauτ which does not make sence.
