Another thing is conditional probability P(A∣B)P(A\vert B)P(A∣B)

P(A∣B)=P(A∩B)P(B)P(A\vert B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)} P(A∣B)=P(B)P(A∩B)​

That’s mean is given event B has occured, what’s the probability that event A occurs?

The original sample space is Ω\OmegaΩ, now we need to revice the sample space to B, do not need to care another area.

if P(B)=0P(B)=0P(B)=0, we call that P(A∣B)P(A\vert B)P(A∣B) is undefined

It also can be represented as follow:

\begin{flalign} P(A\cap B)&=P(B)\times P(A\vert B) \\ &=P(A)\times P(B\vert A)& \end{flalign}

here is the explaination: the probality of A and B occurs is proportional to the probability of B occurs. As long as we do experiments, we will find that when B occurs, sometime A also occurs, proportionally, and the proportional means P(A∣B)P(A\vert B)P(A∣B)
